Our System Was Reading the Warning Signs in Reverse

Last quarter we had $4,855 in confirmed fraud losses. Every single one of those fraudulent transactions was rated "Low Risk" by our own system — meaning our team had no reason to look twice.

Think of it like a smoke alarm that rings loudly when you're making toast — and stays completely silent when there's a real fire. The four signals we rely on most were each pointing the wrong way.
2 / 6

Four Miscalibrated Dials

Signal 1
Suspicious Devices
Transactions from high-risk devices — the kind associated with fraud rings — were being trusted more, not less. Now they correctly raise the risk score.
Signal 2
International Transactions
Cross-border purchases were being treated as safer than domestic ones. Every confirmed fraud case in our data was international. Now they add risk.
Signal 3
Rapid-Fire Activity
When an account makes 6 or more purchases in 24 hours — a classic sign of a compromised account — the system was rewarding it with a lower risk score. Now it raises the alarm.
Signal 4
Prior Fraud History
Accounts with a history of chargebacks were actually receiving lower risk scores — meaning repeat offenders looked cleaner than first-time customers. That's now reversed.

What This Means Day-to-Day

The fraud operations team can now trust the High Risk queue. A transaction flagged high risk has a 100% chargeback rate in our data. A transaction rated low risk has a 0% chargeback rate.

We've also added 81 automated checks that run every time the system is updated. If any of these four signals ever flip back to the wrong direction, the system will catch it before it reaches production — not months later when we're counting losses.
